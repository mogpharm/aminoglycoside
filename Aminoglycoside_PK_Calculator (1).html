Determine Patient-Specific PK from Measured Levels
2 levels: two concentrations let the tool individualize patient PK (Sawchuk–Zaske). A peak + trough in the same interval (no dose between) solves both k and Vd. A trough-before-dose then peak-after-dose, or any pair with a dose between, is also supported (see sampling pattern below).   1 level: Vd fixed at population estimate; solves for k.
Sample timing need not be exact — "peak"/"trough" are used loosely. Once ke is found, the tool reports the true Cmax (end of infusion) and Cmin (just before the next dose) for the regimen, extrapolated from your levels regardless of when they were drawn.

Future Dose Regimen Estimator
Select a proposed dose and interval to estimate steady-state peak & trough.
Model: One-compartment IV infusion, linear superposition.
During infusion: C(t) = (R/Vk)(1−e−kΔt) where R = dose/tinf  ·  After infusion: C(t) = C(tinf) × e−k(t−tinf)
k (Sawchuk–Zaske): k = 0.00293×CrCl + 0.014  ·  Vd: ≈0.25×weight  ·  CrCl (Cockcroft–Gault): ((140−age)×wt)/(72×SCrmg/dL) × 0.85 if ♀
Peak+trough solver: k = ln(Cpk/Ctr)/Δt, then Vd from the infusion equation (both individualized).
SS peak (end-inf): Cmax = (R/Vk)(1−e−k·tinf)/(1−e−kτ)  ·  SS trough: Cmin = Cmax·e−k(τ−tinf)

⚠ Clinical Disclaimer: For educational and clinical decision-support purposes only. All dosing decisions must be reviewed by a licensed pharmacist or physician per institutional protocols. PK models are approximations; individual responses vary. Aminoglycosides carry nephro- and ototoxicity risk — confirm targets, sampling times, and monitoring against your local guidelines.
